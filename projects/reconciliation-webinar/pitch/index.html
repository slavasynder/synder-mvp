Deferred Revenue

The Deferred Revenue Challenge

Money received ≠ money earned — and your GL needs to reflect that

What Is Deferred Revenue?
💵 Customer pays $1,200 upfront for a 12-month subscription
📅 You earn $100/month as the service is delivered
📊 The unearned portion sits in a Deferred Revenue liability account
Synder's RevRec engine handles this automatically — calculating expected balances based on your recognition schedule.
The Verification Problem
How do you know the GL account actually matches what RevRec expects?
Traditional method:
Export RevRec schedule → Export GL trial balance → Paste into spreadsheet → Compare manually → Investigate row by row
⏱️ This takes hours per period and mistakes are easy to miss. The more subscriptions you have, the worse it gets.
🎯 Synder's Deferred Revenue Reconciliation eliminates the spreadsheet step entirely. One click to compare expected vs actual.

Pro Tips

Tips & Best Practices

Get the most out of reconciliation

🔄
Run Regularly

Don't wait until year-end. Weekly or bi-weekly reconciliation catches issues early when they're easy to fix.

🔍
Investigate Early

A small discrepancy now becomes a big problem later. If something looks off, dig in right away while the context is fresh.

📤
Use Export

Export reconciliation results for your records. Great for audit trails and sharing with clients or team members.

↔️
Check Both Sides

Transaction reconciliation shows "Missing in Accounting" AND "Missing in Integration." Both directions matter for complete accuracy.

📅
Reconcile Before Closing

For deferred revenue — always run reconciliation before closing a period. Ensures your revenue recognition is accurate in the GL.

✅

Q&A

Questions? 🙋

Let's answer what's on your mind. Here are some common ones:

Q
Can I reconcile multiple integrations at once?
Transaction reconciliation runs per integration — you select which one to reconcile (e.g., Stripe, Shopify, PayPal). You can run separate reconciliations for each and compare results. Deferred Revenue reconciliation looks at the overall expected vs actual balance across all sources.
Q
When's the best time to run Transaction Reconciliation if I'm on Summary mode?
Before summaries sync to your books. Once a summary lands in your books, you only see one aggregated entry — the individual transaction detail is gone. So this is your one chance to confirm every transaction from your platform actually made it into Synder.
Q
What happens if I change a RevRec schedule mid-period?
Synder recalculates the expected balance based on the updated schedule. The next time you run Deferred Revenue Reconciliation, it will use the new expected values. Any previous reconciliation reports remain as snapshots of that point in time.

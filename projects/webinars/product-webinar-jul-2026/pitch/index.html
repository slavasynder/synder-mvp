Financials — reports on your integration data

A new section in Synder for building reports directly on the data your integrations bring in — sales, refunds, fees, channels, taxes, customers. All in one place, no exports required.

Ready-made templates
Gross Sales, Average Order Value, Gateway Fees, Top 5 Products & Customers, Sales by Channel, Sales by Shipping Country, Refunds by Product, Tax Summary, Income vs Expenses…
Or build your own
Three ways to create a report — from a Template, from Scratch, or With AI. Tables, line charts, pie charts, stacked bars — pick the visual that fits.
Why it matters to you
Answers to the questions accounting doesn't cover — "which channel drove revenue this month?", "what did we lose to gateway fees?", "which products got refunded most?" — built on your Synder transaction data, ready when you open the page.

Sync Speed dashboard — Per Transaction

See how much you're syncing and what's slowing it down — right inside Synder, no Support ticket needed. Available for Per Transaction orgs.

Graph 1 — Volume
Transactions synced per platform, aggregated daily · weekly · monthly · 6mo · 12mo · custom. Filter by platform and status (Failed / Synced / Warnings / Skipped / Canceled).
Graph 2 — Time breakdown
Avg per-transaction sync time, split by process step: integration fetch, QBO customer lookup, RevRec lookup, JE creation… Toggle to drill from group to step level.
Why it matters to you
Answers questions like "how long will it take to sync my 50k historical transactions?" — with your own data, on the spot. And when something feels slow, you can see exactly which step is taking the time.

Automated Transaction Reconciliation — Shopify & Amazon

Reconciliation used to cover Stripe and PayPal. Now Shopify and Amazon join — no file uploads, no column mapping, no spreadsheet.

Works on Per Transaction and Summary Sync
Shopify
Synder pulls the "Transactions and associated payouts" report for you and reconciles your Shopify Payments clearing account against your accounting.
Amazon
Synder pulls your Settlement reports automatically and reconciles them against your accounting.
Old way → new way
Export report · match in a spreadsheet → Pick a date range → Start matching → Matched · Discrepancy · Not Matched · Ignored

A new way to troubleshoot syncs

Sync Issues — troubleshoot by the error, not by the sync

A new tab on your Dashboard that flips how you resolve sync problems — start from the error, fix it once, and resync every transaction it affected.

Old way → new way
Open a failed sync · read the error · fix · next sync · rinse & repeat → Start from the error → Fix once → Resync all affected transactions
Where to find it
The Sync Issues tab on your Dashboard. Every kind of sync failure — failed, canceled, rolled back, and rule failures — grouped into one row per error message.
How you'll use it
Read the error (e.g. "Account not found — 150"), fix the root cause (a missing account, a mapping), open the related transactions, and resync all of them in a single action.
Good to know
Errors are grouped by the exact message, so messages that include a specific number ("total 40 vs 39" vs. "total 41 vs 40") show as separate rows. Batches run up to ~500 transactions at a time, and the list refreshes every hour or so.

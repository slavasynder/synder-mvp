The Basics

Revenue Recognition is…

The GAAP-compliant rule for when revenue actually counts

The Idea

A part of accrual accounting that defines when and how revenue is recognized.

💡 Cash reaching your hand ≠ revenue. Receiving the money and earning it are two different events.
The Standards
ASC 606
United States — FASB standard
IFRS 15
International — IASB standard

Today we focus on SaaS — but the concept applies to any business that collects money upfront for service delivered over time.

The Canonical Example

Cash vs Accrual

Same $1,200 yearly subscription paid in March — two very different revenue pictures

Cash basis — full $1,200 in March
Accrual (GAAP) — $100/month, every month
Same money. Same customer. Accrual reflects reality — you've only earned what you've delivered.

The Foundation

What is Deferred Revenue?

A liability account: money collected, not yet earned. $1,200 invoice → 12 JEs → $0.

On invoice creation
Full $1,200 goes into Deferred Revenue. Nothing into income yet.
On invoice payment
Deferred revenue doesn't change. Cash and revenue are separate events.
Each month, automatically
A journal entry moves $100 from Deferred Revenue → Income.

Recognition Schedule · Method 1 of 3

Daily Ratable

Most precise — every month recognizes its exact share of days. Closest to strict GAAP.

💡 Amounts fluctuate by calendar. May = 23 days only → $75.62, not a full $100.
Recognition Schedule · Method 2 of 3

Monthly Ratable

Straight-line. Divide the invoice amount by the number of months — same amount every month.

✅ Pro: Dead simple to explain. Predictable lines on the P&L.
⚠️ Catch: Sub starts June 30? You still recognize $100 for June — even if only 1 day was delivered. Less accurate at the edges.
Recognition Schedule · Method 3 of 3

Monthly Ratable, Prorated First & Last Months

Best of both worlds: clean middle months, accurate at the edges. Recommended default.

🏆 Synder's recommended default. Clean to read, accurate at the edges, easy to audit.
Use Case 1 · Refunds

Recognition of Refunds

Scenario: $300 three-month sub. $100 recognized in August. Full refund in September.

Full refund

August's $100 has to come out of P&L. You didn't keep the money — you can't keep the revenue.

Partial refund (unused 2 months only)

First month was earned legitimately — nothing to claw back. Only the unused portion gets refunded. Synder handles both.

Use Case 1 · In QuickBooks

Profit & Loss (Refund Example)

August +$100, September –$100. Net zero — exactly correct.

🎯 The customer got their money back. Net of both months you provided no paid service. Books reflect reality.
Use Case 2 · Cancellation

Subscription Cancellation

Cancellations ≠ refunds. As long as service is delivered, recognition continues.

📐 The rule: as long as you're providing the service, you keep recognizing. The schedule doesn't change.
⚠️ Only exception: if you also stop service immediately AND refund the unused portion — that's the refund case (Slide 13).
Use Case 3 · Discounts

Discounted Subscription

$1,200 yearly with a $240 discount → $960 invoice. Discounts recognize the same way as the product.

Principle

Recognize the discount the same way as the product — straight-line, same period, same method.

Result

Clean P&L split between gross subscription revenue and discounts — line-item level.

Use Case 3 · In QuickBooks

Profit & Loss (Discount Example)

Discounts given appears as a separate income line — distinct from Stripe sales.

🔍 Useful for pricing analysis later — no hunting through invoice details to find where the discounts went.

Recognition Through the Modification

Recognize at the product level, not the invoice level — clean audit trail.

Use Case 5 · Recognition Start

Recognition from an Invoice Date

Recognition starts when service starts — not when cash arrives. Default for B2B with net terms.

⏱️ Net-30 / net-60 terms common in B2B. Clock starts when service starts — not when payment arrives.
Use Case 6 · Bad Debt

Uncollectible / Voided Invoices — Bad Debt

$1,200 subscription, July start. Marked uncollectible in September after $121.65 recognized.

🧠 The logic: Jul + Aug service was real — you can't undeliver it. So you don't reverse P&L. Instead, the unrecoverable amount goes to a Bad Debt expense account.
Use Case 7 · Alternative Start

Recognition from a Payment Date

For businesses where service delivery is gated on payment received.

📅 Same principle, applied to a different moment of service start. Recognition kicks off retroactively from the payment date.
Use Case 8 · Multi-line Invoices

Multi-Line Invoices

Customer with two simultaneous subs — yearly $1,200 + 6-month $600. Each on its own schedule.

📊 Best practice: map each product to its own income account → track revenue by product line in your P&L.

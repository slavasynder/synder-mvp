The Problem

Why Reconciliation Matters

The receivable side is fundamentally harder than the payable side — and you know it.

The Receivable Reality

For every one sale, you can have multiple transactions to align — a sales receipt, an expense for the fee, a payout. The matching is many-to-one, by hand, at scale.

It's where the hours stack up and small errors can quietly accumulate before close.
What Transaction Reconciliation Does

Catches the gaps and discrepancies before you close the books, not after. The point isn't auditing — it's pre-close hygiene.

If a transaction is missing, mismatched, or never made it across — you find out while you still have time to fix it cleanly.

Transaction Reconciliation

Resolution Paths Today

Two workflow actions when issues surface, three hygiene checks before you run.

When the result needs action
🛠
Adjust in books → refresh
Fix the discrepancy in QBO/Xero, then refresh the reconciliation. The in-Synder fix is on the roadmap.
⏭
Ignore — acknowledged dismissal
For rows that genuinely shouldn't be matched. The reconciliation math closes around them.
Hygiene checks before you run
🏦
Clearing accounts
Sales, refunds, fees, payouts all land in the same clearing account per integration.
🔄
Sync hygiene
No Ready-to-Sync rows. No failed or cancelled rows. No deleted transactions left unresolved.
⚡
Auto-import on
If auto-import is off, transactions never make it into Synder in the first place.
Clean upstream = clean reconciliation. The hygiene items below the line catch the bulk of "Missing in Accounting" cases before they ever surface.

Concept · No UI Yet · DIS-44

Period Locking & Soft Close

When the books close, Synder respects it. With one variant worth discussing.

🔒
Hard Close

Closed period is closed. A late transaction lands under the earliest open date.

No re-opening. Strict discipline.
🚪
Soft Close

Closed for now — but if something needs to land in the closed period, re-open · enter · close again.

Flexible. Pragmatic.
Both patterns appear in practice — the right default depends on how a firm approaches its close.
